Overall Meaning

In Layton Greene's song "Leave Em Alone," the chorus highlights the trap lifestyle and how it's causing a conflict of interest in her love life. She's trying to settle down and play the girlfriend role, but the temptation of trap boys keeps creeping in. The opening verse takes a sexual turn, with Layton singing about a guy whom she is interested in. The second verse includes the perspective of the guy she finds desirable, rapping about how he's a dealer but that shouldn't prevent them from pursuing a relationship. He mentions his sexual prowess and suggests that the others don’t compare to him.


The lyrics in this song imply an addiction to the trap lifestyle and how the desire for money and fast living can be alluring, directly clashing with the longing for a committed, long-term relationship. Layton Greene brings both perspectives to light and suggests that the singer is making a crucial decision to pursue a particular lifestyle at the cost of her connections with men. The repetition of the chorus highlights the central conflict: she's caught up in a lifestyle that presently holds more attraction than conventional relationships.
